About

I enjoy programming like I enjoyed exploring Tolkien's Middle-earth, cataloguing and rearranging our home library, or even building toy cities as a child. To me, the fun comes from designing a structure, incrementally adding detail and simplifying the arrangement of the parts - and in the end having an elegant "machine", that also magically comes to life and performs seemingly impossible tasks. Whether it's a report-generating app, or a system guiding the next Mars rover - it's the complexity of what lies beneath that makes it so interesting.

As a programmer, most of my work has been (so far) on content management system architecture and, to some extent, generative programming, mostly in the context of web applications. I also wrote research code in a variety of areas, only to discover over and over again that, to me, building the tools for research was by far more exciting than using them to do research and learn more about this or that phenomena. As Fred Brooks famously said, "scientists build to learn, engineers learn to build." Building things is what makes me happy and curious for more knowledge.

I live in beautiful Gaithersburg, MD, with my wife Alina, who is a postdoc at the University of Maryland and is 10 times the academic I ever would've been; and Alice, our little daughter, who loves her books and stuffed animals, and learned the sl (steam locomotive) bash command at age 2 :-)
